About Me

Hello, my name is Amish! I like learning about the world and building stuff that makes it better.

I was born and raised in Northern Virginia, where my first non-family love was planes—probably because I saw one almost every day. I grew up speaking two languages, learned another one in school, played several sports, and tried my hand at a few artistic endeavours (theater, singing, instruments, and painting). I first fell in love with the world of atoms when I represented my middle school and district several times in the You Be the Chemist Challenge. I formally studied chemical engineering, but I like using multidisciplinary tools to solve all scales of problems, from making a vending machine for friends to modeling catalysts that reduce toxic emissions.

Energy and education are the things I think about the most right now, but I also write about other topics in science, technology, and philosophy. I’m always open to feedback and discussion, so feel free to share your thoughts via my contact information below.

Other fun stuff (that won't fit on my resume):

  • Lived on a UNESCO World Heritage site.
  • Did competitive Bollywood fusion dance (and won!).
  • Played D-1 collegiate ultimate frisbee.
  • Averaged under 15 seconds to solve a Rubik’s cube.
  • Taught a college course on frugal engineering.

Things I’ve learned [permanently under construction]:

  • Have conviction in your beliefs. Be data-driven instead of emotionally driven.
  • Fail quickly and learn. Update your priors Bayes’ Theorem-style!
  • Reason from first principles instead of by analogy. Question every assumption.
  • Thrive in uncertainty.
  • Have a bias for action.
  • Surround yourself with high-value people whom you respect.
  • Appreciate that things can be radically different from the way they are now.
  • Define a problem well before solving it.
  • Always keep the big picture in mind. Never lose the forest for the trees.
